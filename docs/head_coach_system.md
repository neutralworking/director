# HEAD COACH SYSTEM DESIGN
## Based on repo research: CoreGameLoop.md, design-document.md, staffResponsibilities.json

---

## üéØ CORE PRINCIPLE
**The Head Coach is your most important hire. Your success is their success.**

- **70% of league position comes from: Manager Quality + Squad Quality**
- Director chooses the coach, sets objectives, provides resources
- Coach handles day-to-day: tactics, team selection, man management, media

---

## üìä COACH ATTRIBUTES (1-20, FM-Style)

### **Tactical (How they set up the team)**
- **Tactical Knowledge**: Understanding systems, formations, styles
- **Match Preparation**: Preparing for specific opponents
- **Adaptability**: Changing tactics mid-match

### **Man Management (Handling people)**
- **Man Management**: Player relationships, morale
- **Motivating**: Team talks, getting best from squad
- **Discipline**: Maintaining standards, dealing with problems

### **Judgement (Making decisions)**
- **Judging Player Ability**: Picking right starting XI
- **Judging Potential**: Developing young players

### **External (Media & Pressure)**
- **Media Handling**: Press conferences, protecting players
- **Pressure Handling**: Performing in crises

### **Training (Improving players)**
- **Attacking Coaching**: Teaching offensive play
- **Defending Coaching**: Teaching defensive structure
- **Fitness Coaching**: Physical conditioning

## üé≠ COACH ARCHETYPES

### **The Tactician** (Guardiola)
‚úÖ Perfect tactical setups, wins through strategy  
‚ùå Over-complicates, poor man-management  
**Best for**: Clubs with intelligent, technical players

### **The Motivator** (Klopp)
‚úÖ Players run through walls, incredible comebacks  
‚ùå Burnout risk, tactical naivety  
**Best for**: Clubs needing energy and belief

### **The Disciplinarian** (Conte)
‚úÖ Organized, hard to beat, clear standards  
‚ùå Players fear him, stifles creativity  
**Best for**: Clubs with discipline problems

### **The Youth Developer** (Arteta)
‚úÖ Young players develop rapidly, long-term value  
‚ùå Short-term results suffer, veterans ignored  
**Best for**: Clubs with great academies

### **The Pragmatist** (Mourinho)
‚úÖ Gets results, wins big games, manages pressure  
‚ùå Boring football, players feel stifled  
**Best for**: Clubs needing immediate results

### **The Idealist** (Bielsa)
‚úÖ Beautiful football, passionate fan favorite  
‚ùå Philosophy over results, exhausts players  
**Best for**: Patient boards, passionate fanbases

### **The Firefighter** (Allardyce)
‚úÖ Survival specialist, calms crises  
‚ùå Limited ceiling, won't win trophies  
**Best for**: Relegation battles

### **The Rookie** (Young Manager)
‚úÖ Fresh ideas, high potential, cheap  
‚ùå Inexperienced, makes mistakes  
**Best for**: Risk-taking directors

## ‚öΩ TACTICAL PHILOSOPHIES

| Philosophy | Requires | Strengths | Weaknesses |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Possession-Based** | Passing, Vision, Technique | Control games, patient | Vulnerable to counters |
| **Counter-Attacking** | Pace, Dribbling, Finishing | Lethal on break | Struggles vs low blocks |
| **High Press** | Work Rate, Stamina | Win ball high, suffocate | Burnout, space in behind |
| **Defensive Block** | Positioning, Tackling | Hard to break down | Boring, low scoring |
| **Total Football** | Intelligence, Stamina | Fluid, unpredictable | Requires elite players |
| **Long Ball** | Height, Strength, Heading | Direct, physical | Limited against good teams |
| **Fluid Attacking** | Versatility, Creativity | Exciting, flexible | Inconsistent |

## ü§ù DIRECTOR-COACH RELATIONSHIP

### **Three Key Metrics:**

1. **Relationship** (0-100): How much they like you
   - Affected by: Meeting objectives, getting requested players, respecting autonomy
   
2. **Trust** (0-100): How much they believe in you
   - Affects: Cooperation, honesty about problems, working together
   
3. **Power Struggle** (0-100): Fighting for control
   - High = Constant conflict over transfers, tactics, media

### **Influence Level** (0-100)
Determines who has more power:

**High Influence Coach:**
- Transfer requests prioritized
- Hard to fire
- Controls team selection completely
- Media listens to him more

**Low Influence Coach:**
- Director controls more
- Easier to replace
- Must accept director's signings
- Media questions his authority

## üìÖ COMMUNICATION & MEETINGS

### **Meeting Types:**
1. **Post-Match Analysis** (After every match)
   - Coach explains performance
   - Director can question tactics/selections
   - Bad results = tension builds

2. **Monthly Squad Discussion**
   - Overall squad health
   - Player development updates
   - Morale concerns

3. **Pre-Transfer Window Requirements**
   - Coach lists positions needed
   - Player types required
   - Priority signings
   - *Influence affects how many requests you must meet*

4. **Crisis Management** (Triggered by events)
   - Losing streaks
   - Player revolts
   - Media pressure
   - Board ultimatums

### **Communication Styles:**
- **Collaborative**: Works with you (easiest)
- **Demanding**: Heavy requirements (challenging)
- **Defensive**: Blames others (frustrating)
- **Transparent**: Honest about problems (valuable)

## üéØ SETTING OBJECTIVES

Director + Board set coach objectives at start of season:

### **League Position** (Most Important)
- Target: Top 4, Top 6, Top Half, Survival
- Weight: 40% of performance review

### **Playing Style** (Board Preference)
- Exciting Football
- Defensive Stability
- Youth Development
- Club Identity

### **Cup Performance**
- Reach Semi-Final
- Win Trophy
- Prioritize/Deprioritize

### **Youth Integration**
- Give debuts to X academy players
- Develop specific prospects

### **Squad Harmony**
- Maintain morale above X
- No dressing room issues

## üíº HIRING PROCESS

### **Step 1: Job Requirements**
Does coach meet minimum standards?
- Club reputation vs Coach reputation
- Budget available
- Facilities quality
- Board ambition

### **Step 2: Tactical Fit**
Score (0-100) based on:
- Does squad suit his philosophy?
- Do we have players for his formation?
- Can he work with current talent?

### **Step 3: Interview**
- Coach states his vision
- You negotiate objectives
- You discuss transfer budget
- You set power balance

### **Step 4: Contract**
- Salary (based on reputation)
- Length (1-5 years)
- Release clause (can other clubs poach?)
- Performance bonuses

---

## ‚ö†Ô∏è FIRING & REPLACEMENT

### **When to Fire:**
- Objectives failed badly
- Lost dressing room (players revolt)
- Board demands it
- Better option available

### **Consequences:**
- Severance payment (contract value)
- Squad morale hit
- Media scrutiny
- Harder to hire next coach (reputation hit)

### **Replacement Options:**
1. **Promote Assistant** (cheap, familiar, but limited)
2. **Hire Experienced** (expensive, proven, but demands power)
3. **Take Risk on Rookie** (cheap, high ceiling, but unproven)
4. **Steal from Rival** (expensive release clause, board loves it)

## üìà PROGRESSION & DEVELOPMENT

Coaches improve attributes over time through:
- **Experience**: Seasons completed ‚Üí +1-2 to key attributes
- **Success**: Winning trophies ‚Üí Reputation boost
- **Courses**: Send to coaching courses ‚Üí Specific attribute gains
- **Mentorship**: Learn from veteran coaches

Coaches can also **regress**:
- Long losing streaks ‚Üí Confidence drops
- Burnout ‚Üí Motivation/Energy decreases
- Age ‚Üí Physical attributes decline

## üîë KEY TAKEAWAYS

1. **Coach choice is THE most important decision you make**
2. **Tactical fit with squad matters more than raw attributes**
3. **Relationship management is crucial - don't overstep**
4. **Set realistic objectives based on squad quality**
5. **Communication frequency affects cooperation**
6. **Power struggles destroy clubs - find balance**
7. **Sometimes you need to fire a good coach for a better fit**

---

*This system creates real tension: Do you hire the proven winner who demands control, or the promising rookie you can mold? Do you back your struggling coach or cut him loose? Do you force your signing on him or trust his judgment?*

**That's the heart of being Director of Football.**
